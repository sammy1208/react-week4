Chapter 1

Celebrities can be unbearably obnoxious. Most seem to walk around with a sense of invincibility, as if fame and wealth grants them dominion over everything and everyone. And the way their fans worship them only served to inflate their already massive egos.

Every celebrity Kiyoomi had encountered so far carried themselves like they were handpicked by the gods.

He despised them.

So why in the hell had he chosen to become the manager of one of Japan’s most loved ex-child-star, current biggest pop stars and notorious fuckboys?

Simple.

The money was just too good to pass up, especially for a freshly graduate with no previous experience on the field.

Still, no amount of cash could make dealing with Miya Atsumu any less infuriating.

Atsumu was the definition of arrogance. He was an entitled brat and insufferably rude to everyone. Every day with him was a battle, and he seemed determined to make Kiyoomi’s life a living hell.

Scandals were a regular occurrence, whether it was careless remarks in front of rolling cameras (because Atsumu really does love running his big stupid mouth), picking public fights with other artists on social media, or some new controversy popping up every other week because the man seemed to have no brain to mouth filter whatsoever.

He wasn’t inherently a bad person; Kiyoomi knew that well. He had witnessed the depth of Atsumu's heart in many occasions, especially when it came to causes that truly mattered. He had leveraged his wealth and fame to help people in need countless times. Rather that was making donations or spreading awareness about important social or political cases.

He had a good heart. The only problem was that it laid buried under many layers of assholness and cockiness. Yet despite these redeeming qualities, he remained trapped in a cycle of poor choices and self-destructive behavior.

Wherever Atsumu went, chaos followed, and Kiyoomi was left scrambling to clean up the wreckage.

It had only been five months since Kiyoomi had taken on the job as his manager, but he was already at his breaking point. At just 23, he swore he could feel grey hairs creeping in, all thanks to the stress the pop-star caused him on a daily basis.

It truly was exhausting being his manager.

Chapter 7: Epilogue

One Year Later

Atsumu couldn’t have asked for a more caring, sweet, and devoted boyfriend—except maybe during those moments when Kiyoomi slipped into full-on manager mode, insisting Atsumu stick to schedules and stay on top of his work. But even then, Atsumu didn’t mind. Life was good.

Over the past year, Atsumu embarked on a Japan tour, which, thanks to the massive success of his album, eventually expanded into a world tour. Every new song he released seemed to go viral, catapulting his career to heights he had never dreamed of.

Atsumu was indeed back and stronger than ever.

 

Kiyoomi met Mama Miya a few months after they had gone official— two months to be specific. After way too many requests from her, Atsumu finally gave in and decided to take his boyfriend back to Hyogo to meet his mother. Needless to say, she had fallen in love with Kiyoomi almost instantly. And to Atsumu’s surprise, Kiyoomi loved her just as instantly.

For Kiyoomi, those days spent with the Miyas were like a revelation—warm, accepting, and filled with the kind of love he hadn’t felt in years. It was a stark contrast to the cold distance he’d grown accustomed to from his own family.

One evening, as they sat together in the quiet comfort of Atsumu's childhood home, Kiyoomi broke down in tears, burying himself in Atsumu’s arms. Through his sobs, he asked why his own family couldn’t be like this—why his mother, father, and siblings couldn’t offer the same unconditional love he was experiencing now.

Atsumu held him tightly, running his fingers soothingly through Kiyoomi's hair as he let the tears fall. He didn’t try to shush him or tell him it would be okay; Atsumu knew better than to offer hollow reassurances. Instead, he whispered softly, “I don’t know why, Omi, but ya deserve this. Ya deserve to feel loved unconditionally like this. And if it helps, I’m sure they still love ya. In their own fucked up way, sure, but still...”

Kiyoomi clung to him, the raw emotion pouring out after years of keeping it locked away. When his sobs finally quieted, Atsumu pulled back slightly, just enough to look into his boyfriend’s tear-streaked face. “Listen,” he said, voice steady but filled with conviction. “No matter what ya’ve got me. And ya’ve got Mom, Osamu, and Rin. We’re yer family too, and we’re not going anywhere.”

Kiyoomi blinked at him, his dark eyes shimmering with unshed tears.

“I love you, Atsumu” he whispered between sobs.

“I love ya too baby,” said Atsumu kissing his forehead.

 

The next morning, Atsumu’s mother seemed to sense the shift. Without a word, she set an extra cup of green tea (his favorite) in front of Kiyoomi at breakfast and gently patted his shoulder as she passed. It wasn’t much, but the quiet, maternal gesture made Kiyoomi’s chest tighten. He realized he had grown to love these little moments of warmth, the ones he’d never thought he could have.

Later that day, as they prepared to leave, Atsumu’s mother pulled Kiyoomi into a firm hug. “Yer always welcome here,” she said softly. “I mean that. With or without Tsumu, come over whenever ya feel like it, k?”

Kiyoomi froze for a moment, unsure how to respond, but then his arms came up to return the hug. “Thank you,” he murmured, his voice thick with emotion.

 

As they drove back home, Kiyoomi sat quietly in the passenger seat, his gaze fixed on the passing scenery. The golden hues of the setting sun played across his face, softening his usually stoic expression. Atsumu stole a glance at him and, unable to resist, reached over to squeeze his hand.

Kiyoomi turned to him, a faint smile tugging at the corners of his lips. “Thanks,” he said softly.

Atsumu tilted his head, eyebrows raising slightly. “Fer what?”

“For everything,” Kiyoomi murmured, his voice barely above a whisper.

Atsumu chuckled, his grin widening. “That’s my line, Omi. I should be thanking you.”

Without letting go, he laced their fingers together, lifting Kiyoomi’s hand to his lips. He pressed a gentle kiss to his knuckles, his eyes warm and affectionate as they flicked up to meet Kiyoomi’s.

Kiyoomi’s faint smile deepened, his cheeks tinged with the faintest blush. “Idiot,” he muttered, but the word was laced with so much love it barely sounded like an insult at all.

 

 

 

 

About eight months into their relationship, Atsumu had finally had enough of his fans—and everyone else—openly thirsting over his man. So, on a random Tuesday, in real Atsumu style, he took matters into his own hands. He decided to put an end to it in the most him way possible.

He posted a photo of Kiyoomi to his Instagram story. The image was casual yet flattering. The caption read:

“Please stop thirsting over and flooding my manager’s DMs. He’s a taken man. Kiyoomi is in a happy, committed relationship. His partner doesn’t appreciate all this. Don’t get the man (and me as his client) in trouble with his lover.”

Since he couldn’t outright say the partner was him, Atsumu had to get a little creative with his wording.

Predictably, the internet exploded. Kiyoomi—and thousands of others—saw the story within minutes. News outlets reported on the “mystery lover” who had stolen the heart of the hot-untouchable manager Kiyoomi Sakusa (who had now, despite his dread for it, become some sort of celebrity himself). Fans mourned the loss of a promising bachelor, and while chaos reigned online, Atsumu couldn’t have been happier.

Kiyoomi sighed, rubbing his temple as he scrolled through his phone. “You’re absolutely ridiculous,” he muttered, though his tone carried more amusement than annoyance.

Atsumu only grinned, tugging Kiyoomi closer. “Yer mine. I told ya, I don’t share.”

Kiyoomi rolled his eyes but let Atsumu pull him into his arms anyway. He didn’t say it out loud, but a small, smug part of him liked how fiercely Atsumu claimed him.

“What should I say, then? You’ve got millions of people thirsting over you every day,” Kiyoomi teased.

Atsumu snorted, his arms tightening around Kiyoomi’s waist. “Yeah, but that's my job I can't really help it.” He pulled Kiyoomi even closer, burying his face in his hair and pressing a soft kiss to the back of his neck. “And besides, yer the only one who gets to put up with my annoying ass.”

Kiyoomi rolled his eyes again, but a quiet laugh escaped him. “That’s one way of putting it.” He glanced down at his phone, still scrolling through the flood of notifications. “You’ve really stirred the pot, though. People are already speculating about who my ‘partner’ is. Some of them think it’s you. Smart people... But this could be a problem for your career. Gay relationships aren’t exactly welcomed in Japan.”

Atsumu shrugged, unfazed. “If it comes out and they figure out we’re dating, then fine. I don’t give a shit. I’m not hiding you or myself anymore. We’ll deal with whatever comes our way.”

Kiyoomi gave him a look, still concerned. “And what if the backlash hurts your image?”

Atsumu flashed a mischievous grin. “Who cares? Besides, they don’t know for sure. Let ‘em speculate. Like Kiyoko always says, there’s no such thing as bad publicity. In a way, all this speculation will just drive more people to my content. As long as nothing’s confirmed to be true, this is just like the K-idol’s fan service, no?”

Kiyoomi sighed, but a smile tugged at his lips. “I guess you’re right, yeah.”

 

 

 

 

Speculation about their relationship continued to swirl and grew more persistent when photos of Atsumu and Kiyoomi leaving restaurants, Cafes, museums or Atsumu’s apartment together started leaking. Atsumu’s team (more like Kiyoko, since the woman was like a one.woman army) quickly stepped in, managing the situation by feeding the media a carefully crafted story about the "strongest bromance"/ “best of buddies” anyone had ever seen. Most of the press bought into it, and the narrative stuck.

Fans, however, weren’t so easily fooled. They knew. They could see the way Atsumu looked at Kiyoomi, the way Kiyoomi’s presence seemed to calm him. An overwhelming majority of fans rallied behind #SAKUATSU (that’s what they called them online), showing their support for the couple despite the media's and Atsumu’s team’s best efforts to spin a different story.

Atsumu couldn’t help but feel a sense of pride. His fans weren’t the homophobic pieces of shit he feared they might be—well, most of them weren’t. And that, at least, was something worth holding onto.

Yet despite the growing support from his fans, the pressure of keeping their relationship under wraps was starting to wear on both of them. The leaks kept coming, and every time they went out in public together, they felt like they were walking a tightrope, always looking over their shoulders.

 

 

 

The news of their romantic relationship finally broke when someone (whom Atsumu vowed to find and sue so thoroughly they’d regret ever crossing paths with him) posted a picture of them kissing at Osamu and Rin’s wedding.

They had let their guard down, swept up in the enchanting atmosphere of love that the wedding exuded, and allowed themselves a moment of unguarded affection.

Kiyoomi was pacing the living room like a madman, panic etched into every step, while Atsumu remained seated on the couch, his eyes glued to the incriminating photo.

“This is bad,” Kiyoomi muttered, his voice tight with worry.

“Hey, calm down, will ya? I’ll handle this,” Atsumu said, his tone steady.

“HOW?!” Kiyoomi let out a sharp exhale, his mind racing. “Atsumu, this isn’t just about us. Do you realize how much is at stake here? Your career, your contracts, endorsements, your fanbase—everything you’ve built.”

“I said I’ll figure it out, ya need to calm down. Seriously yer hyperventilating right now”

Kiyoomi glared at him. “The industry isn’t kind to… situations like this. How the fuck will you cover this up? Blame A.I?”

“Maybe? I mean, technology has gotten like crazy good lately,” shrugged Atumu.

“Atsumu I’m serious!” said Kiyoomi sounding exasperated. Atsumu sighed and got up.

He stepped closer, his expression softening. “If ya think I’m gonna let the fear of some backlash keep me from livin’ my life—our life—then ya don’t know me as well as ya think.”

Kiyoomi sighed, pinching the bridge of his nose. “Atsumu…listen,”

“No, ya listen to me,” Atsumu interrupted, his voice growing firm. “I’ve spent years playin’ by the rules, bein’ what everyone wanted me to be. But what’s the point of all that if I can’t be happy? If I can’t be with the person who makes me wanna keep waking up and do all this in the first place? I don’t give a damn about my career if keepin’ it means losin’ ya. Got it? If I have to choose between the two, it’s you. Every single fucking time. No questions asked.”

Kiyoomi’s shoulders sagged, the fight slowly draining out of him. “But that’s not fair, baby. I don’t want you to lose everything because of me. Atsumu this is your dream we’re talking about. You can’t lose it now that it’s finally going great for you,” he admitted, his voice barely above a whisper.

“Lose everything…my dream? Baby,” Atsumu repeated, stepping forward until they were only inches apart, “bein’ with you is my dream right now. You are what makes me happy, losin’ ya would mean losing everything,”

“Yeah, there’ll be backlash at first. People will talk, and call me names, maybe my views and streams will dip, and some brands might drop me. But I won’t back down. I’ll keep doin’ what I always do, bein’ so much myself, so much in their faces, shove it so much down their throats, until they won’t have a choice but to accept it. “

Kiyoomi looked at him, tears glistening in his eyes, before nodding firmly.

“Of course I am,” he said.

Atsumu smiled and pulled him into a tight hug.

“You’re really sure about this…?” whispered Kiyoomi.

“Never been more sure in my life,”

No impulsive stunts, no reckless announcements. We’ll come out on our terms, with a plan that protects your career and us as much as possible.”

“It’s my job,” Kiyoomi replied, narrowing his eyes. “And don’t think I’m letting you off the hook just because this is personal. You’re still my client, and I expect you to follow my lead.”

“Yes, sir,” Atsumu said with a mock salute, his grin mischievous.

Despite himself, Kiyoomi smiled, a quiet resolve settling in his chest. Whatever the fallout might be, they’d face it together.

 

 

 

 

Two years later

 

"Will you marry me?" Kiyoomi asked, kneeling at the very restaurant where they’d shared their first date all those years ago.

Proposing to Atsumu hadn’t felt like a monumental decision—because, in Kiyoomi’s heart, it wasn’t. It was simple, inevitable. There was no one else but Atsumu.

Atsumu was his person, the one he wanted by his side for the rest of his days. He was the love of Kiyoomi’s life.

He couldn’t imagine loving anyone the way he loved Atsumu. And, honestly, he didn’t even want to.

"Omi... yes! Fuck yes, yes, yes!" Atsumu exclaimed, overwhelmed with joy, as Kiyoomi slid the ring onto his finger.

Tears welled up in Atsumu’s eyes as he threw himself into Kiyoomi’s arms, nearly knocking them both to the ground. The restaurant erupted in applause, a mix of strangers and their close friends who had secretly been invited to witness the proposal. Osamu was wiping at his eyes with a napkin, pretending he wasn’t crying, while Suna snapped a discreet photo from across the table along with Motoya (the two were a perfect match made in hell when it came to embarrassing Atsumu and Kiyoomi).

Kiyoomi held Atsumu tightly, his own cheeks tinged pink as the emotions threatened to overwhelm him. He wasn’t one for grand gestures or public displays of affection, but for Atsumu, he’d gladly step outside his comfort zone.

“Yer stuck with me now. Like fer real,” Atsumu whispered against his ear, his voice trembling with happiness.

“I’ve been stuck with you for a while now, Atsumu,” Kiyoomi murmured back, his lips quirking up in a soft smile. “And I wouldn’t have it any other way.”

 

 

 

 

Atsumu broke the news of their engagement on Instagram, with a photo of their hands, both wearing their matching rings and then another photo of them kissing. The caption read.

“To the haters - Are ya mad bruh?<3”

And some people were mad, the slurs and hate comments flooded his post, but they didn’t matter, not when most of his fans and people who engaged with his post were beaming and fangirling/boying over their relationship. And with time and therapy, Atsumu learned to ignore the negative comments and focus on the positive ones.

As the weeks went by, their engagement became a lightning rod for both controversy and celebration. Media outlets tried to milk the story for all it was worth—debating, speculating, and often sensationalizing every detail. But Atsumu’s fans, the loyal core who had supported him through thick and thin, rallied around the couple with unwavering enthusiasm.

Soon, the conversation began to shift. Instead of questioning or condemning, many were inspired by their openness. They became accidental advocates for queer representation, their love story serving as an example for countless fans who had struggled with their own identities.

One particularly touching moment came a few months later, during the Tokyo leg of Atsumu’s latest tour. Just before his encore, the lights dimmed, and a montage of fan letters and video messages played on the massive screen behind him. Messages of gratitude, hope, and love poured in—many from LGBTQ+ fans who thanked Atsumu (and Kiyoomi) for showing them that they, too, deserved happiness.

Standing center stage, microphone in hand, Atsumu’s eyes shimmered with unshed tears. He glanced toward the wings, where Kiyoomi stood just out of view, offering him a small, reassuring nod. Atsumu turned back to the crowd, his voice thick with emotion as he said, “Thank ya. Thank ya for acceptin’ me, for supportin’ us. It means more than I could ever put into words. I love ya, all of ya.”

The audience erupted in cheers, their applause a thunderous wave of support. As Atsumu launched into his final song, Kiyoomi watched from backstage, his heart swelling with pride. Atsumu was shining brighter than ever, not despite their love, but because of it.

From that moment on, it felt as though the tides were shifting. Slowly but surely, society seemed to follow suit, embracing the change that had once felt impossible.

Atsumu was happy.

But, of course, not every moment was a fairytale. Some days were grueling, especially when battling the relentless pull of addiction. On his worst days, Atsumu feared he might relapse and undo all the progress he’d made—jeopardizing his career, his relationship, and his hard-won happiness. But Kiyoomi was always there, holding his hand through the darkness. So were Osamu, Suna, and his mother, standing steadfastly by his side.

Although he never relapsed, the fear of falling back lingered. Therapy remained a constant in his life, and Mina continued to guide him through his darkest moments, helping him confront the fear and paranoia that occasionally crept in.

At 28, Atsumu felt a happiness he once thought impossible. For the first time, he felt truly seen—and deeply, unconditionally loved.

 

 

 

 

“Do you, Sakusa Kiyoomi, take Miya Atsumu to be your husband, in sickness and in health, till death do you part?”

“And do you, Miya Atsumu, take Sakusa Kiyoomi to be your husband, to cherish in sickness and in health, for as long as you both shall live?”
